Gait Rehabilitation System

Team-driven design for stroke rehabilitation through 5-DOF robotic control.

Abstract

Stroke remains a leading cause of long-term disability, often impairing mobility and balance. Rehabilitation is critical to restoring motor function, particularly through gait training and postural re-education. This two-phase project leverages robotic platforms to create a more adaptive, precise, and customizable rehabilitation system for stroke patients. Phase 1Conceptual Development and Kinematic Redesign: To overcome limitations of traditional Stewart platformsâ€”namely restricted workspace and susceptibility to singularitiesâ€”this phase introduces a hybrid architecture: a 3RPS (Revolute-Prismatic-Spherical) parallel leg manipulator mounted on a 2D Cartesian gantry. This decouples translational and rotational motions, allowing independent control of leg height, angle, and position. Dual joysticks provide manual control while an onboard IMU adjusts pitch for real-time orientation feedback. A Propeller Activity Board coordinates synchronized multi-servo motion. Future iterations will implement spline-based motion control and preloaded trajectories from an SD card for smoother and programmable movements. Phase 2Clinical Application and Rehabilitation Integration: Building on the modular platform from Phase 1, this phase adapts the robotic system for clinical stroke rehabilitation, focusing on balance training and gait assessment. The parallel manipulator's capacity for precise, multi-axis motion enables programmable, repeatable movement patterns that can be tailored to patient needs. By simulating uneven terrains and dynamic balance conditions, the system offers a controlled environment for retraining motor skills and postural stability. The modularity also supports integration with feedback systems for real-time monitoring and therapist supervision. Keywords : Parallax Propeller, Gait rehabilitation, IMU, Robotics, Stroke rehabilitation
